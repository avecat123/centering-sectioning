About UWM

The University of Wisconsin-Milwaukee provides an affordable, world-class education to 27,500 students from 91 countries. Its 15 schools and colleges include Wisconsin’s only schools of architecture, freshwater sciences and public health.

Academics

UWM offers 194 degree programs with classes taught by faculty members recognized worldwide as experts in their fields. It also has Wisconsin’s largest online education program with more than 850 classes.

Read more about Academics at UWM.

Research

As one of only two top-tier research universities recognized in Wisconsin, UWM plays a critical role in discovery and innovation.

Groundbreaking Discoveries

Research focuses on solving global problems such as sustainable energy, health care initiatives and protecting the Great Lakes.

Student Involvement

Students can work with faculty members on research projects across many disciplines.

Community Partners

UWM partners with thousands of businesses and nonprofits to support community engagement.

Read more about Research at UWM.

Get to Know Milwaukee

Milwaukee sits on Lake Michigan and blends big-city energy with a friendly small-town feel. The city celebrates culture with festivals and invests in future innovation.
